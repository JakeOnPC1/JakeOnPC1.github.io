⚡ Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics describes nature at the smallest scales – atoms and subatomic particles. Core concepts:

  • Wave-particle duality – particles (e.g., electrons) behave as waves and particles.
  • Superposition – a particle can exist in multiple states until measured.
  • Entanglement – linked particles instantly affect each other across distance.
  • Heisenberg uncertainty principle – you cannot know both position and momentum exactly.

Schrödinger's equation describes how quantum states evolve. Quantum theory is essential for lasers, transistors, and MRI.

🔬 The famous double‑slit experiment shows wave‑particle duality.
