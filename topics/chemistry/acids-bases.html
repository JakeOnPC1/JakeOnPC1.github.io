🧴 Acids & Bases

Acids donate protons (H⁺), bases accept them (Brønsted‑Lowry). pH scale: 0–14, 7 neutral.

  • Strong acids – HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃ (fully dissociate).
  • Weak acids – acetic acid (vinegar).
  • Strong bases – NaOH, KOH.
  • Neutralization – acid + base → salt + water.
  • Indicators – litmus (red/blue), phenolphthalein.

pH = -log[H⁺]. Buffer solutions resist pH changes – crucial for biology.

💡 Stomach acid is ~0.5 M HCl (pH ~1.5).
