🔗 Chemical Bonding

Atoms bond to achieve stable electron configurations (like noble gases). Three main types:

  • Ionic bonds – transfer of electrons (e.g., NaCl). High melting points, conduct electricity when dissolved.
  • Covalent bonds – sharing electrons (e.g., H₂O, CH₄). Can be polar or nonpolar.
  • Metallic bonds – "sea of electrons" in metals (conductivity, malleability).

Intermolecular forces (hydrogen bonding, van der Waals) affect physical properties like boiling point.

🧪 Example: Water's high boiling point comes from hydrogen bonding.
